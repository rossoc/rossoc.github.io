## Project Concept

Apply advanced numerical homogenization methods to efficiently solve multiscale problems in computational mechanics. The goal is to develop robust, scalable techniques that capture fine-scale effects while maintaining computational efficiency.

---

## Key Techniques to Explore

### 1. **Multiscale Finite Element Method (MsFEM)**

Framework for constructing coarse-grid basis functions that capture fine-scale features. Includes basis function enrichment strategies and local refinement techniques.

### 2. **Multigrid-based Homogenization**

Hierarchical grid approaches with coarse-grid correction for homogenized problems and accelerated convergence for multiscale PDEs.

### 3. **Localized Orthogonal Decomposition (LOD)**

POD-based multiscale enrichment with local basis function computation and orthogonal decomposition for error control.

### 4. **Bayesian Numerical Homogenization**

Uncertainty quantification in homogenized parameters with probabilistic framework for material parameter estimation and Bayesian inference for microstructure characterization.

### 5. **Optimal Local Multi-scale Basis Functions**

Greedy selection of important fine-scale features, adaptive basis enrichment, and error-driven basis optimization.

### 6. **Iterative Numerical Homogenization**

Fixed-point iteration schemes, Newton-type methods for homogenization, and convergence acceleration techniques.

- Heterogeneous materials (composites, porous media)

- Computational homogenization of periodic structures
